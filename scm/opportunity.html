African Opportunities

Unleashing the Potential of a Vibrant Market

Africa is the world's second-largest and second-most-populous continent, with a population of over 1.3 billion people, and a landmass of 30.2 million square kilometres surpassing that of the United States, China, India and Europe collectively. Africa represents a land of opportunity, with a growing middle class, abundant natural resources and a youthful population that is increasingly tech-savvy and entrepreneurial. By 2030, young Africans are expected to constitute 42% of global youth – World Economic Forum, translating to dynamism in investing and unique innovations. The continent has economic potential, with several sectors experiencing significant growth currently. With expansions across technology, agriculture, e-commerce and renewable energy, these industries are driven by factors such as; increased mobile and internet penetration rates, a growing youthful population and technological advancements. In 2022, mobile phone subscribers stood at 46% in Sub-Saharan Africa, while smartphone adoption was at 64% and expected to expand to 50% and 75% by 2025 – GSMA report. Overall, the growth of these sectors represents a significant opportunity for businesses and investors looking to tap into the African market. At Sthenic, we are strategically positioning ourselves to explore these opportunities and unlock growth.

Tapping into a Resilient Private Debt Market

Africa's private debt market is rapidly growing, with assets under management (AUM) of over $15 billion, according to the African Private Equity and Venture Capital Association. Despite this growth, the private debt market in Africa is still relatively untapped, offering significant opportunities for investors. Fundraising for private debt funds in Africa increased signif icantly, with $2.3 billion raised in 2021, up from $1.3 billion in 2020. This suggests that investors are increasingly interested in the African private debt market. Although the total value of these deals also decreased from $1.4 billion in 2020 to $1.1 billion in 2021, we are still optimistic about this market. Despite some inherent risks in the African private debt market, the market in Africa presents a wealth of opportunities. With a growing middle class, an expanding consumer base, and a range of untapped industries, the growth potential is significant. We also expect the long-term growth prospects for the continent to remain strong, backed by the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) agreement that is expected to boost cross-border trade and investment. We are walking this road with investors and high margins f irms to drive positive change in Africa, promote value investing, support sustainable businesses, and create jobs and opportunities for local communities.
